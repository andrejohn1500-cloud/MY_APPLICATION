## Level 11
1. The concept of 'muscle fibre recruitment' → **The nervous system activates more motor units as exercise intensity increases**
2. 'Oxygen debt' (EPOC) after exercise → **Extra oxygen consumed to restore the body to its resting state**
3. The concept of 'cardiac hypertrophy' in athletes → **The heart muscle enlarges and strengthens through regular endurance training**
4. 'Stroke volume increases' with training because → **The heart becomes stronger and pumps more blood per beat**
5. The concept of 'blood redistribution during exercise' → **Blood is redirected from non-essential organs to working muscles**
6. 'Venous return' is aided by → **Muscle contractions, breathing and venous valves**
7. The concept of 'respiratory adaptations to training' → **Increased lung capacity, stronger respiratory muscles and more efficient gas exchange**
8. 'Mitochondrial density increases' with training → **More mitochondria in muscle cells improve aerobic energy production**
9. The concept of 'capillarisation' → **Training increases the number of capillaries supplying muscles with oxygen**
10. 'Haemoglobin concentration' increases with → **Endurance training and altitude exposure**
11. The concept of 'thermoregulation during exercise' → **The body sweats and increases blood flow to skin to prevent overheating**
12. 'Blood pooling' after exercise can cause → **Dizziness if the person stops suddenly without cooling down**
13. The concept of 'the anaerobic threshold' → **The exercise intensity at which lactic acid accumulates faster than it can be cleared**
14. 'Muscle buffering capacity' allows → **Athletes to tolerate higher lactic acid levels before fatigue**
15. The concept of 'neuromuscular fatigue' → **Both the muscles and nervous system contribute to reduced performance**
